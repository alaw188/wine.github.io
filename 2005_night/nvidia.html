2005 Burgundy & Barolo Vertical Tasting

An exploration of six exceptional wines from legendary vintages, showcasing the pinnacle of terroir expression from Burgundy's Côte d'Or and Piedmont's Langhe region.

The 2005 Vintage: A Climatological Triumph

The 2005 growing season in both Burgundy and Piedmont presented near-ideal climatic conditions: a cool, wet winter replenished groundwater reserves; a warm, dry spring promoted even budbreak and flowering; and a long, sunny summer with minimal disease pressure allowed for phenolic ripeness to develop alongside vibrant acidity. These conditions yielded wines of remarkable structure, depth, and longevity—attributes particularly evident in the structured, tannic framework of Nebbiolo and the pinot noir's ability to retain both fruit purity and earthy complexity.

This tasting examines six premier cru and grand cru expressions from esteemed producers, each illustrating the nuanced interplay of site-specific viticulture and vintage character.

Burgundy Vineyard Locations

The featured Burgundy wines originate from three distinguished communes within the Côte d'Or: Nuits-Saint-Georges and Vosne-Romanée in the Côte de Nuits, and Corton in the Côte de Beaune. Each commune imparts distinct geological and microclimatic influences.

Barolo Wine Region: Ginestra

The Barolo wine originates from the Ginestra vigneto within the Barolo DOCG zone of Piedmont, Italy. Situated in the comune of Monforte d'Alba, Ginifera benefits from a south-southeast exposure and marl-rich sandstone soils, contributing to the wine's powerful structure and aromatic precision.

Featured Wines

Domaine Méo-Camuzet
2005 Nuits-Saint-Georges 1er Cru Aux Boudots
Situated on the upper slopes of Nuits-Saint-Georges, Aux Boudots benefits from deep, marl-limestone soils and optimal southern exposure. Widely regarded as the most Vosne-like of the Nuits premier crus due to its location immediately below Vosne-Romanée's Aux Malconsorts and its marl-enriched soils.

Tasting Notes & Serving Guidance:

Typical Style: Aux Boudots usually combines Nuits structure with Vosne perfume: think refined tannin, red-to-dark cherry fruit, floral lift, and more aromatic finesse than a typical NSG. Collectors often treat it as a bridge wine between Nuits and Vosne.

What to Expect from Your Bottle: For your 2005 Méo Boudots, expect something potentially silky but still serious: floral and spice notes if it's open, with enough structure that it may need some time in glass.

Site Details: 6.31-hectare Premier Cru at the northernmost end of Nuits-Saint-Georges. East-southeast-facing slope at 250–285 meters elevation. Soils are marl-rich stony loam over Bathonian limestone.

Service: Gentle decant 30 minutes prior to serving; serve at 15–18°C.
Domaine Méo-Camuzet
2005 Vosne-Romanée 1er Cru Les Chaumes
Les Chaumes occupies a premier cru site in Vosne-Romanée characterized by its iron-rich limestone and clay soils. The Méo-Camuzet bottling comes from a producer associated with finesse and elegance, even if their wines can take time to open.

Tasting Notes & Serving Guidance:

Typical Style: Within a lineup like yours, Les Chaumes is generally the bottle that shows the most obvious Vosne character: aromatic, supple, spicy, and seductive earlier than the Nuits wines, while still having 2005 backbone.

What to Expect from Your Bottle: This is likely the most fragrant, least structurally severe, and best candidate to drink first in your lineup. That said, because it's still 2005 red Burgundy, it may open reserved and then gain perfume with air.

Aroma Profile (from notes): Black fruits, game, cassis, elderberry, beet root, salty stony minerality, savory soy and meat juice.

Palate (from notes): Firm but ripe, rich in mouth, striking length, will become silky with age.

Service: Decant 1 hour before serving; serve at 15–18°C.
Domaine Méo-Camuzet
2005 Nuits-Saint-Georges 1er Cru Aux Murgers
Located on the northern boundary of the Nuits-Saint-Georges commune, Aux Murgers is a 4.89-hectare Premier Cru named for the "murger" dry-stone walls built from rocks cleared from the vineyard. The site is southeast-facing around 250 meters elevation with deep, rocky Comblanchien limestone and free-draining soils.

Tasting Notes & Serving Guidance:

Typical Style: Aux Murgers tends to be elegant rather than heavy, with bright black fruit, spice, floral lift, minerality, silky tannins, and fresh acidity. Often described as another Nuits cru that bridges toward Vosne in polish.

What to Expect from Your Bottle: Among the Méos, this one may be a touch more mineral, tensile, and inward than Chaumes at first. Expected to reward air with a more linear, aristocratic profile.

Site Details: Vines planted in 1965 and 1972 according to tastingbook.com, with grapes that ripen early while retaining striking acidity.

Service: Gentle decant 30 minutes; serve at 15–18°C.
Domaine de Montille
2005 Corton Grand Cru Clos du Roi
As a monopole within the Corton Grand Cru hill, Clos du Roi enjoys a unique microclimate and shallow, limestone-rich soils. The Hill of Corton is unusual in Burgundy for its amphitheater form and broad range of exposures spanning Aloxe-Corton, Ladoix-Serrigny, and Pernand-Vergelesses.

Tasting Notes & Serving Guidance:

Typical Style: Official regional guidance describes red Corton as intense, velvety crimson, with aromas of fruit, violet, and with age: underbrush, leather, pepper, and liquorice. On the palate: well-built, powerful, muscular, chewy, sensual, and structured.

What to Expect from Your Bottle: Your 2005 de Montille Corton Clos du Roi is likely the broadest-shouldered wine here: more power, more Corton authority, and probably the one most likely to need the longest runway. It's the bottle to save for last in the lineup.

Site Details: Hill elevation 250–330 meters with southeast to southwest exposures. Red Corton has reddish, pebbly soils derived from limestone with marl and high potassium content.

Service: Decant 45 minutes; serve at 16–18°C.
Domaine Henri Gouges
2005 Nuits-Saint-Georges 1er Cru Les Chaignots
Les Chaignots is a historic premier cru in Nuits-Saint-Georges noted for its clay-limestone soils and proximity to the Vosne-Romanée border. The wine delivers a firm, structured profile with savory, farmyard nuances and excellent aging potential.

Tasting Notes & Serving Guidance:

From tasting notes: Farmy nose, positive fruit, built structure. Full red colour, good fruit that built and built, excellent with cheeseboard.

Serving Recommendation: Decant 1 hour; serve at 15–18°C.

Service: Decant 1 hour; serve at 15–18°C.
Domenico Clerico
2005 Barolo Ciabot Mentin Ginestra DOCG
Ginestra, a premier cru within Monforte d'Alba, features a southeastern exposure and marl-rich calcareous clay soils. The 2005 displays opulent dark fruit, floral lift, and impeccable balance between powerful tannins and vibrant acidity—a benchmark expression of the site.

Tasting Notes & Serving Guidance:

Aroma Profile: Dark plums, full bloom red rose, spices, sweet spices, menthol, violets, dark fruit.

Palate: Great acidity, medium tannins, ruby/purple color, endless layers of sweet spices.

Serving Recommendation: Decant 2–3 hours; serve at 16–18°C.

Service: Decant 2–3 hours; serve at 16–18°C.
